Latest Scam Alert

Phishing Email Scam Targeting Bank Customers

Alert Date: October 15, 2023

Scammers are sending fraudulent emails pretending to be from major banks, asking customers to verify their account details due to "suspicious activity." These emails contain links to fake banking websites designed to steal login credentials.

Key Indicators:

  • Urgent language demanding immediate action
  • Generic greetings like "Dear Customer" instead of your name
  • Suspicious sender email addresses
  • Links that don't match the bank's official website

Protect Yourself

Banking Security: Do's and Don'ts

Do:

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each financial account
  • Enable two-factor authentication whenever possible
  • Regularly monitor your account statements
  • Keep your contact information updated with your bank
  • Use secure networks for financial transactions

Don't:

  • Share passwords or PINs with anyone
  • Click on links in unsolicited emails or texts
  • Use public Wi-Fi for banking without a VPN
  • Save banking passwords in your browser
  • Respond to calls asking for personal information

Security Software & Device Protection

Keeping your devices secure is crucial for protecting your financial information.

Essential Security Measures:

  • Install reputable antivirus and anti-malware software
  • Keep your operating system and apps updated
  • Use a firewall to block unauthorized access
  • Enable device encryption for added protection
  • Regularly back up important data

Recommended Update Schedule:

  • Operating System: Update immediately when available
  • Antivirus Software: Daily automatic updates
  • Browser: Weekly checks for updates
  • Banking Apps: Update as soon as new versions are released

What Banks Do to Protect You

Financial institutions employ multiple layers of security to safeguard your accounts.

Security Measures Implemented by Banks:

  • One-Time Passwords (OTPs) for transaction verification
  • Encryption of all sensitive data in transit and at rest
  • Fraud detection systems monitoring for suspicious activity
  • Secure socket layer (SSL) certificates for website security
  • Multi-factor authentication for account access

Your Role in Security:

While banks provide robust security measures, customers must also practice safe banking habits to ensure comprehensive protection.

Digital Fraud

Identity Theft

Identity theft occurs when someone uses your personal information without your permission to commit fraud or other crimes.

Common Examples:

  • Opening credit cards or loans in your name
  • Filing tax returns using your Social Security number
  • Using your health insurance to obtain medical care
  • Creating fake IDs using your personal information

Phishing

Phishing is a cybercrime where targets are contacted by email, telephone, or text message by someone posing as a legitimate institution to lure individuals into providing sensitive data.

Common Examples:

  • Fake banking emails requesting password verification
  • Social media messages with malicious links
  • Fake invoice attachments containing malware
  • Impersonation of tech support to gain remote access

Vishing

Vishing (voice phishing) involves fraudulent phone calls or voice messages pretending to be from reputable companies to induce individuals to reveal personal information.

Common Examples:

  • Automated calls claiming your Social Security number is compromised
  • Callers pretending to be from your bank's fraud department
  • Tech support scams requesting remote access to your computer
  • Warrant scams threatening arrest unless payment is made

Smishing

Smishing (SMS phishing) uses text messages to trick victims into providing personal information or downloading malicious software onto their mobile devices.

Common Examples:

  • Texts claiming you've won a prize and need to click a link
  • Messages about suspicious account activity with a link to "verify"
  • Fake delivery notifications requiring payment or information
  • COVID-19 related scams offering tests or financial relief

SIM Swap & Porting Fraud

SIM swapping occurs when a fraudster convinces your mobile carrier to transfer your phone number to a SIM card in their possession, allowing them to intercept two-factor authentication codes.

How It Works:

  • Scammers gather personal information through data breaches or phishing
  • They contact your mobile provider pretending to be you
  • They claim your SIM card is lost or damaged and request a replacement
  • Once they control your number, they can bypass SMS-based 2FA

ATM Fraud

ATM fraud involves various techniques criminals use to steal your card information or cash at automated teller machines.

Common ATM Fraud Techniques:

  • Skimming devices attached to card readers
  • Hidden cameras recording PIN entries
  • Trapping devices that prevent card retrieval
  • Distraction techniques while transactions are in process

How the Scam Works

Step-by-Step Breakdown:

  1. Scammers send emails that appear to be from legitimate banks
  2. Emails create urgency by claiming suspicious account activity
  3. Recipients are directed to click a link to "verify" their account
  4. The link goes to a fake website that mimics the real bank's login page
  5. Victims enter their credentials, which are captured by scammers
  6. Scammers use these credentials to access real bank accounts

Protect Yourself:

  • Never click links in unsolicited emails
  • Always navigate to your bank's website directly
  • Check for HTTPS and security certificates
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
  • Regularly monitor your account statements

Identity Theft Safety Tips

Prevention:

  • Shred documents containing personal information before disposal
  • Use strong, unique passwords for all online accounts
  • Monitor your credit reports regularly
  • Be cautious about sharing personal information online
  • Freeze your credit if you suspect your information is compromised

If You're a Victim:

  • Contact the fraud departments of the three major credit bureaus
  • Place a fraud alert on your credit reports
  • File a report with the Federal Trade Commission
  • Contact your financial institutions to secure accounts
  • File a police report
